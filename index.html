Deaths in Malaysia in 2022

Death rates are a crucial indicator of public health, influenced by a variety of factors including population demographics.

In Malaysia, these rates vary significantly across different states and regions, providing broader challenges faced by the population.

We also dive deeper into specific causes of deaths.

The leading cause of death in 2022 was

circulatory diseases, which is

43.88% out of 131,687 deaths.

This is recorded by Ministry of Health Malaysia from MOH

and private hospitals.

Malaysia saw a rise in heart disease and stroke during 2013, and a significant spike in death rates by 2019 due to non-communicable

diseases and healthcare pressures.

Gender impacts life expectancy, with women generally

living longer than men due to:

  • Biological Factors: Genetic advantages and protective hormones.
  • Health Behaviors: Men engage in riskier behaviors and seek healthcare less.
  • Lifestyle: Women tend to eat healthier and engage in more health-promoting activities.

Not only mortality is affected by gender, it is also affected by ethnics in Malaysia.

The boxplot above shows that the highest death rates belong to Men that are Bumi(Malay) while the lowest belongs to Women that are

Non-Citizen. This proves the fact that women has lower death rates than men.
